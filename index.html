About Myself

Hello! I’m Aditi Thakur, a dedicated medical graduate from Lalitpur, Nepal, with an MBBS degree from Universal College of Medical Sciences and Teaching Hospital (UCMS), Bhairahwa, Nepal. My medical journey has been deeply shaped by my passion for community service, where I’ve had the privilege of working closely with underserved populations in Nepal. I’ve been involved in numerous healthcare initiatives, from organizing medical camps to providing essential services in rural areas, which have strengthened my commitment to making quality healthcare accessible to those in need. These experiences have not only sharpened my clinical skills but have also instilled in me a deep sense of responsibility toward improving public health. In addition to my community work, I’ve completed observerships at UCI Irvine in California and Trident Hospital in Charleston, which have broadened my understanding of diverse healthcare systems. I actively seek out opportunities to learn and grow, participating in medical conferences to stay current with advancements in the field. My greatest drive comes from my desire to contribute to the well-being of communities, and I remain dedicated to making a lasting impact through compassionate care and service.
